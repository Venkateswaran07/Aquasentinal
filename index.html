Scientific Methodology

1 Satellite Data Acquisition

We utilize Sentinel-2 MSI (Multi-Spectral Instrument) data from the Copernicus program. The system automatically filters for the least cloudy images (< 20% cloud cover) within the last 90 days for current analysis, and aggregates seasonal data (Winter, Summer, Monsoon) from the previous year.

2 Water Surface Extraction (NDWI)

The Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) is calculated using the Green (B3) and Near-Infrared (B8) bands:
NDWI = (Green - NIR) / (Green + NIR)
Pixels with NDWI > 0.1 are classified as water. This dynamic thresholding allows for precise delineation of water body boundaries.

3 Volumetric Estimation Model

Since satellite imagery only provides surface area, we estimate volume using a Conical Depression Model augmented by terrain slope.

  • Surface Elevation: Derived from NASADEM superimposed on water pixels.
  • Slope Analysis: Surrounding terrain slope is calculated to estimate the depression angle ($$\theta$$).
  • Depth Estimation: $$Depth = Radius \times \tan(\theta)$$
  • Volume: $$V = \frac{1}{3} \times Area \times Depth$$

4 Bathymetric Mapping

Relative depth is visualized by subtracting the underlying DEM elevation from the calculated average water surface elevation.
Depth_Pixel = Water_Surface_Elev - Terrain_Elev
This generates a gradient map showing estimated deep and shallow zones.
